# OrphanTracker Imagery & Illustration Guidelines

## Visual Language Overview

OrphanTracker's imagery reflects our core mission: **compassion, hope, and community care**. Our visual language combines:
- **Warm, inclusive photography** showing real people and authentic moments
- **Simple, approachable illustrations** that demystify complex social issues
- **Consistent color application** using our brand palette
- **Human-centered composition** that builds emotional connection

---

## Photography Style Guide

### Brand Photography Principles

#### Authenticity
- **Real people**: Feature actual orphans, caregivers, and community members
- **Genuine moments**: Candid photos showing real interactions and emotions
- **Diverse representation**: Show children and families of various ethnic backgrounds, abilities, and family structures
- **Avoid**: Stock photos, overly posed scenes, artificial perfection

#### Warmth & Hope
- **Natural lighting**: Golden hour, soft daylight, warm tones
- **Intimate framing**: Close-ups of faces, hands, connections between people
- **Positive energy**: Smiling faces, supportive physical contact, active engagement
- **Color grading**: Warm white balance, slight saturation boost, subtle vignette

#### Dignity & Respect
- **Ethical imaging**: Always obtain consent, especially with vulnerable populations
- **Avoid patronizing imagery**: No "savior complex" photos (white volunteers helping)
- **Show agency**: Children and caregivers actively engaged, not passive victims
- **Privacy**: Never show children in vulnerable states; always show them thriving

#### Inclusivity
- **Multiple identities**: Gender, ability, ethnicity, age, family structure
- **Community focus**: Show community networks, not just individuals
- **Intersectionality**: Represent overlapping identities (e.g., disabled girls, older boys)

---

### Photography Use Cases

#### Hero Section
- Large, impactful image (1600×900px minimum)
- Show children learning, playing, or being cared for
- Bright, optimistic composition
- Overlay with semi-transparent color (purple at 40% opacity) to reduce contrast
- Text should be readable with white text + dark text shadow

**Example**:
```
Image: Children in classroom laughing during lesson
Overlay: rgba(102, 126, 234, 0.4)
Text: White, 56px, bold, centered
```

#### Card Images
- Square or 4:3 ratio (400×300px minimum)
- Focus on specific impact areas: education, health, shelter, family support
- Clear subject with blurred background (20px blur acceptable)
- Consistent saturation and warmth across all cards

**Categories**:
- **Education**: Classroom scenes, tutoring moments, achievement celebrations
- **Health**: Medical checkups, nutrition activities, play-based wellness
- **Shelter & Care**: Safe, clean living spaces, supportive caregiver interactions
- **Family Reunification**: Children with family members, community gatherings

#### Testimonial/Story Photos
- Portrait photos of individuals sharing their story
- 1:1 ratio or portrait orientation
- Soft background (out of focus or blurred)
- Natural expression, genuine emotion
- Consider cultural context and comfort level

#### Data/Impact Graphics
- Photographs can accompany statistics
- Show real examples of impact (before/after scenarios acceptable if ethical)
- Infographic integration: overlay numbers, icons on photos
- Maintain dignity while showing transformation

---

### Photography Technical Specifications

#### Lighting
- **Color Temperature**: 3500-4500K (warm daylight)
- **Shadow Softness**: 30-50% fill light to reduce harsh shadows
- **Avoid**: Fluorescent lighting (5000K+), extreme backlighting
- **HDR**: Slight HDR processing acceptable for dramatic skies (subtle, not obvious)

#### Composition
- **Rule of Thirds**: Position subjects on intersecting grid lines
- **Leading Lines**: Use natural lines to guide viewer's eye
- **Depth**: Include foreground, subject, background for dimensional feel
- **Framing**: Use natural frames (doorways, windows) to focus attention

#### Color Grading
- **Saturation**: +10-20% (warm and vibrant, not oversaturated)
- **Contrast**: +5-15% (defined but not harsh)
- **Shadows**: Lifted by 10-15% (reduce harsh blacks)
- **Highlights**: Preserved at 100% (bright but not blown out)
- **White Balance**: Warm (3600-4000K preset)

#### Resolution & Format
- **Minimum Resolution**: 2x display size (e.g., 1200px for 600px display)
- **Format**: JPEG (quality 85+) for web, PNG for transparency
- **File Size**: Optimized under 500KB for web use
- **Aspect Ratios**: 
  - Hero: 16:9 (1600×900)
  - Card: 4:3 (400×300)
  - Portrait: 1:1 (400×400)
  - Banner: 21:9 (1200×515)

---

### Photography Do's & Don'ts

| ✅ DO | ❌ DON'T |
|-------|----------|
| Show real children and communities | Use stock photos of "poverty porn" |
| Capture genuine emotional moments | Stage overly posed scenes |
| Represent diverse ethnicities and abilities | Show all children as helpless or sad |
| Include supportive adults and caregivers | Emphasize the "savior" volunteer |
| Display children thriving and learning | Photograph children in distress |
| Show community cooperation and networks | Focus on individual hardship |
| Use warm, natural lighting | Apply heavy filters or unrealistic colors |
| Get informed consent for all photos | Use children's images without permission |

## Illustration Style Guide

### Illustration Principles

#### Simplicity
- **Clean lines**: 2-4px strokes (adjust for size)
- **Minimal details**: Focus on essential shapes
- **Flat design**: Avoid gradients, 3D effects, heavy shadows
- **Scalability**: Icons should work at 16px and 256px

#### Approachability
- **Friendly shapes**: Rounded corners (8-16px radius), soft curves
- **Human proportions**: Stylized but recognizable
- **Expressive faces**: Simple dots/arcs for eyes/mouth convey emotion
- **Warmth**: Use brand color palette, avoid cold grays

#### Consistency
- **Unified stroke weight**: Same line thickness across illustration set
- **Consistent proportions**: Similar-scale characters and objects
- **Cohesive color palette**: Use 3-5 colors per illustration
- **Visual harmony**: Icons should "feel" like they belong together

---

### Illustration Components

#### Icons (16px - 64px)
- **Usage**: Feature icons, navigation, status indicators, educational graphics
- **Style**: Outline style with 2px strokes (scale proportionally)
- **Colors**: Primary purple (#667eea), accent green/amber, or single color
- **Examples**:
  - Education icon: Book with pencil
  - Health icon: Heart or medical cross
  - Safety icon: Shield
  - Care icon: Two hands supporting

**SVG Template** (24px icon):
```xml
<svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" viewBox="0 0 24 24" width="24" height="24">
  <path stroke="#667eea" stroke-width="2" fill="none" d="..." stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"/>
</svg>
```

#### Illustrations (200px - 600px)
- **Usage**: Section headers, empty states, onboarding flows, educational content
- **Style**: Outlined with filled shapes, 2-4px strokes
- **Composition**: Simple scenes with clear subject and minimal background
- **Colors**: 3-5 colors from brand palette per illustration

**Common Subjects**:
- Children learning and playing
- Caregivers showing affection
- Community gathering
- House/shelter symbolism
- Education symbols (books, pencils)
- Health symbols (hearts, medical items)
- Growth metaphors (plants, upward arrows)

#### Character Illustrations
- **Style**: Simple, friendly, age-appropriate
- **Diversity**: Show various ethnicities, abilities, family structures
- **Flexibility**: Modular design allows mixing components (heads, bodies, expressions)
- **Usage**: Onboarding, team pages, testimonials, educational content

---

### Illustration Color Application

#### Primary Illustrations
- **Base color**: Primary purple (#667eea)
- **Accent 1**: Accent green (#10b981) for positive elements
- **Accent 2**: Accent amber (#f59e0b) for highlights
- **Neutral**: Gray (#9ca3af) for outlines, secondary shapes
- **Background**: White or transparent

**Example**: Child reading book
- Child clothes: #667eea
- Book: #10b981
- Light rays: #f59e0b
- Face/outline: #1a1a1a

#### Dark Background Illustrations
- **Base**: Light purple (#a78bfa) or white
- **Accents**: Bright green, amber (more saturated)
- **Outline**: White or light gray
- **Background**: Dark semi-transparent overlay

---

### Illustration Technical Specifications

#### Stroke Properties
```xml
<svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg">
  <style>
    .icon-stroke {
      stroke-width: 2;
      stroke-linecap: round;
      stroke-linejoin: round;
      fill: none;
    }
    .icon-fill {
      fill: #667eea;
      stroke: none;
    }
  </style>
</svg>
```

#### Sizing
- **Favicon**: 16×16px, solid fill
- **Icon (small)**: 24×24px, outline
- **Icon (large)**: 64×64px, outline
- **Illustration**: 200-600px, multiple strokes/fills
- **Typography**: SVG text should be 16px+ for readability

#### File Format
- **SVG**: Preferred (scalable, small file size)
- **PNG**: Fallback (export at 2x resolution with transparency)
- **Optimization**: Remove metadata, compress with SVGO

---

### Illustration Use Cases

#### Feature Illustrations (Hero/Section)
- Large central illustration (300×300px or larger)
- Represents main benefit/value
- 3-5 color palette
- Often paired with section heading

**Example Sections**:
- "Why OrphanTracker?" → Illustration of children + caregiver + safe environment
- "How We Help" → Multi-person scene showing care chain
- "Your Impact" → Growth metaphor (plant growing from heart)

#### Empty State Illustrations
- Medium size (150-200px)
- Friendly, encouraging tone
- Communicates the empty state (no data, no results)
- Often paired with CTA: "Let's get started!"

**Example Empty States**:
- No complaints filed: "Help us make a difference"
- No orphanages added: "Discover organizations to support"
- No donations yet: "Join our giving community"

#### Educational Graphics
- Explain complex concepts visually
- Step-by-step illustration (4-6 steps)
- Clear numbering and flow
- Combines icons + scenes

**Examples**:
- "Complaint Process": Report → Review → Action → Follow-up
- "Child Development": Physical → Emotional → Social → Educational milestones
- "Our Services": Identification → Placement → Support → Reunification

#### Onboarding Illustrations
- One illustration per step
- Progressive complexity (simple → detailed)
- Celebrates user progress
- Friendly, encouraging tone

**Steps** (example):
1. Welcome: Friendly child + heart
2. Browse: Child with notebook
3. Support: Hands helping
4. Impact: Growing plant

## Photography & Illustration Integration

### Grid Layout with Mixed Media
When combining photos and illustrations:
- **Alternation**: Photo, illustration, photo pattern
- **Consistency**: Maintain visual weight and color harmony
- **Breathing room**: Use spacing (16-32px) between elements
- **Color cohesion**: Ensure photo colors harmonize with illustration colors

### Overlay Techniques
```css
/* Semi-transparent color overlay on photos */
.photo-overlay {
  position: relative;
  background: linear-gradient(135deg, rgba(102, 126, 234, 0.3) 0%, rgba(118, 75, 162, 0.4) 100%);
  mix-blend-mode: multiply;
  border-radius: 8px;
  overflow: hidden;
}

/* Text overlay with text shadow */
.photo-text {
  position: absolute;
  color: white;
  text-shadow: 2px 2px 8px rgba(0, 0, 0, 0.3);
  font-size: 2.25rem;
  font-weight: 700;
}
```

### Icon + Illustration Pairing
- Illustrations can contain embedded icons
- Icons should follow same style guidelines
- Use icons as emphasis/pointers within larger illustrations

## Sourcing Images & Creating Illustrations

### Photography Sources
- **Preferred**: Own photographs (ethical, authentic, controlled)
- **Licensed Stock**: Unsplash, Pexels (diverse, real people)
- **Avoid**: Istock, Gettyimages (artificial, stock-look)
- **Ethical Considerations**: Always obtain consent, especially with vulnerable populations

### Illustration Tools
- **Vector**: Adobe Illustrator, Figma, Affinity Designer, Inkscape (free)
- **Raster**: Photoshop, GIMP (if exporting to PNG)
- **SVG Online**: draw.io, Boxy SVG, Excalidraw (quick sketches)

### Design Resources
- **Color Palette**: Use brand CSS variables
- **Typography**: System font stack in SVG text
- **Accessibility**: Add `<title>` and `<desc>` tags to SVGs

## Accessibility for Imagery

### Alt Text for Photos
```html
<!-- Good -->
<img src="children-reading.jpg" alt="Five children from diverse backgrounds reading books together in a bright classroom">

<!-- Bad -->
<img src="children-reading.jpg" alt="image">
```

### SVG Accessibility
```xml
<svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" role="img" aria-labelledby="icon-title">
  <title id="icon-title">Education Icon</title>
  <path d="..."/>
</svg>
```

### Color Contrast for Illustrations
- Background + illustration colors: 3:1 minimum
- Use patterns in addition to color for status indicators
- Test with colorblind simulation tools

### Responsive Images
```html
<picture>
  <source media="(max-width: 767px)" srcset="image-mobile.jpg">
  <source media="(min-width: 768px)" srcset="image-desktop.jpg">
  <img src="image-fallback.jpg" alt="Descriptive alt text">
</picture>
```
