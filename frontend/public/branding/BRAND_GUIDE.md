# Ujyalo Bhavishya Abhiyan - Brand Guidelines

**Tagline:** Safe Children. Bright Futures.  
**Nepali:** सुरक्षित बालबालिका। उज्यालो भविष्य।

## 1. Brand Identity

### Mission
Ujyalo Bhavishya Abhiyan (उज्यालो भविष्य अभियान) is a Nepal-focused child protection initiative dedicated to monitoring, reporting, and protecting vulnerable children through community engagement and institutional accountability.

### Core Values
- **Safety First:** Protect every child from exploitation and harm
- **Transparency:** Hold orphanages and institutions accountable
- **Empowerment:** Enable communities to report and act
- **Hope:** Build a bright future for Nepal's vulnerable children
- **Compassion:** Treat every child with dignity and respect

### Brand Personality
- **Warm & Approachable:** Compassionate tone without being patronizing
- **Professional & Trustworthy:** Rigorous reporting, credible impact
- **Action-Oriented:** Encouraging participation and engagement
- **Cultural Pride:** Celebrating Nepal's heritage and identity
- **Modern & Accessible:** Easy to use for all Nepali speakers

## 2. Visual Identity

### Logo

#### Primary Logo
- **File:** `ujyalo-logo-primary.svg`
- **Usage:** All main applications (website, social media, documents)
- **Layout:** Horizontal format with mandala icon (left) + text (right)
- **Colors:** Full-color (Deep Red, Gold, Saffron)
- **Minimum Size:** 200px width (maintains legibility)
- **Clear Space:** At least 20px on all sides
- **Background:** Works on white or light backgrounds

#### Icon/Mark
- **File:** `ujyalo-logo-icon.svg`
- **Usage:** Favicon, app icon, social avatars, small spaces
- **Size:** 256×256px (square)
- **Colors:** Deep Red, Gold, Saffron (mandala centered)
- **Simplification:** Child silhouette center, lotus petals frame
- **Minimum Size:** 64px width

#### Monochrome Logo
- **File:** `ujyalo-logo-monochrome.svg`
- **Usage:** Printing, embroidery, black & white media
- **Colors:** Black text, black mandala on white
- **Context:** Used when color printing is unavailable or restricted
- **Clear Space:** 20px minimum

### Logo Don'ts

❌ **Never:**
- Stretch or distort the logo
- Recolor the logo (use monochrome variant instead)
- Rotate the logo at angles other than 0°/90°/180°/270°
- Add shadows, gradients, or effects
- Change the proportion of icon to text
- Use on backgrounds that don't provide sufficient contrast
- Remove the gold border or decorative elements
- Scale the logo below 200px (use icon mark instead)

✅ **Do:**
- Maintain the original aspect ratio
- Use adequate clear space around the logo
- Select the appropriate version (primary/icon/monochrome)
- Ensure sufficient contrast with background
- Test logo visibility at different sizes
- Use the provided SVG files (avoid rasterization)

## 3. Color Palette

### Primary Colors

#### Deep Red (#B31217)
- **Meaning:** Safety, protection, vitality
- **Usage:** Primary buttons, headings, key elements
- **Ratio:** 30-40% of design

#### Gold (#F5C542)
- **Meaning:** Prosperity, enlightenment, hope
- **Usage:** Accents, borders, highlights, decorative elements
- **Ratio:** 15-20% of design

#### Saffron (#FF8C00)
- **Meaning:** Wellness, courage, sacrifice
- **Usage:** Secondary accents, illustrations, background fills
- **Ratio:** 10-15% of design

### Neutral Colors

#### Dark Background (#0B0B0B)
- **Usage:** Text, dark mode backgrounds, emphasis
- **Contrast:** 20.3:1 on white (WCAG AAA)

#### White (#FFFFFF)
- **Usage:** Main background, card surfaces, clear space
- **Ratio:** 40-50% of design

#### Light Gray (#F0F0F0)
- **Usage:** Subtle borders, dividers, disabled states
- **Ratio:** 5-10% of design

### Color Applications

| Element | Color | Hex | Usage |
|---------|-------|-----|-------|
| Hero gradient | Deep Red → Saffron | #B31217 → #FF8C00 | Page headers, banners |
| Primary CTA | Deep Red + Gold | #B31217 / #F5C542 | Call-to-action buttons |
| Text emphasis | Deep Red | #B31217 | Headings, important text |
| Decorative borders | Gold | #F5C542 | Logo trim, accent lines |
| Success state | Green | #10B981 | Form validation, confirmations |
| Error state | Deep Red | #B31217 | Error messages, warnings |
| Link color | Deep Red | #B31217 | Hyperlinks (with underline) |

## 4. Typography

### Typeface Selection

#### English Headings
- **Font:** Montserrat
- **Weights:** 600 (SemiBold), 700 (Bold), 800 (ExtraBold)
- **Example:** "Ujyalo Bhavishya Abhiyan"

#### Nepali Text
- **Font:** Noto Sans Devanagari
- **Weights:** 400 (Regular), 600 (SemiBold), 700 (Bold)
- **Example:** "सुरक्षित बालबालिका"

#### Body Text
- **Font:** Open Sans (English), Noto Sans Devanagari (Nepali)
- **Weight:** 400 (Regular)
- **Size:** 16px (1rem) minimum for readability

### Type Hierarchy

| Level | Size | Weight | Usage |
|-------|------|--------|-------|
| **Display (H1)** | 2.5rem | 700 | Page titles, hero sections |
| **Heading (H2)** | 2rem | 700 | Section headings |
| **Subheading (H3)** | 1.5rem | 600 | Card titles, category headers |
| **Lead** | 1.125rem | 400 | Introductory paragraphs |
| **Body** | 1rem | 400 | Main content (1.6 line height) |
| **Small** | 0.875rem | 400 | Captions, metadata |

## 5. Tone of Voice

### Brand Voice Attributes

#### Compassionate
- Use language that shows empathy for children's situations
- Avoid clinical or detached language
- **Example:** "Every child deserves safety and care" (not "Children require protection protocols")

#### Professional
- Demonstrate credibility through proper citations and data
- Use precise, clear language
- **Example:** "2,850+ children monitored in Nepal" (with sources)

#### Action-Oriented
- Encourage participation and engagement
- Use active verbs and clear CTAs
- **Example:** "Report a concern today" (not "You may report concerns")

#### Culturally Respectful
- Acknowledge Nepal's heritage and context
- Use appropriate Nepali terminology
- Respect local customs and sensitivities
- **Example:** "Supporting Nepal's vulnerable children" (not "Saving Nepal's poor children")

#### Hopeful
- Balance severity with optimism
- Focus on solutions and positive impact
- **Example:** "Together, we're building a brighter future for Nepal's children"

### Writing Guidelines

❌ **Avoid:**
- Patronizing or condescending tone
- Overly emotional or sensationalist language
- Political or religious bias
- Clichés or stereotypes about Nepal/Asia
- Jargon without explanation

✅ **Do:**
- Use active voice and clear structure
- Include specific data and examples
- Address the reader directly
- Celebrate wins and progress
- Respect cultural context

### Example Messaging

| Context | Example |
|---------|---------|
| Homepage hero | "Safe Children. Bright Futures. Join us in protecting Nepal's most vulnerable." |
| Complaint filing | "Your report matters. Help us protect children in your community." |
| Donation page | "Every contribution strengthens our impact. Together, we protect Nepal's children." |
| About section | "Ujyalo Bhavishya Abhiyan monitors orphanage standards and investigates child welfare concerns." |

## 6. Imagery & Visual Style

### Photography Direction

#### Subject Matter
- **Focus:** Children (in safe, dignified contexts only)
- **Settings:** Classrooms, community centers, safe environments
- **People:** Real Nepali children and families (with proper consent/release)
- **Activity:** Learning, playing, engaging in positive activities

#### Visual Qualities
- **Warm Tones:** Golden hour lighting, warm color grading
- **Human Connection:** Close-ups showing faces, emotions, relationships
- **Authentic:** Real moments, not staged or overly posed
- **Respectful:** Never exploitative or sensationalist
- **Diverse:** Represent Nepal's diverse communities and contexts

#### Color Overlay
- **Technique:** Use purple/deep red gradient overlay on images
- **Opacity:** 20-30% for readability
- **Purpose:** Create visual cohesion with brand colors

### Illustration Style

#### Design Approach
- **Simplicity:** Clean lines, minimal detail
- **Accessibility:** Colorful, easy to understand
- **Cultural:** Incorporate Nepali patterns and motifs
- **Warmth:** Use brand color palette (Deep Red, Gold, Saffron)

#### Use Cases
- Icons (security, reporting, monitoring, community)
- Explanatory graphics (how to report, impact timeline)
- Decorative elements (section dividers, headers)
- Data visualization (charts, infographics, statistics)

### Visual Examples

✅ **Good Image Examples:**
- Child studying with a teacher
- Community group discussion
- Safe environment (school, center)
- Hands together in solidarity
- Happy, smiling children in appropriate contexts

❌ **Avoid:**
- Sad or distressed children
- Explicit scenes of abuse or harm
- Stereotypical "poor orphan" imagery
- Over-processed or heavily filtered photos
- Images without proper consent

## 7. Design System

### Component Guidelines

#### Buttons
- **Primary:** Deep Red background with white text
- **Hover:** Slightly darker red with lift effect
- **Sizes:** Large (CTA), Normal (standard), Small (secondary)
- **States:** Default, Hover, Active, Disabled, Loading

#### Cards
- **Style:** Glass effect with subtle shadow
- **Colors:** White background with gold or red accent border
- **Spacing:** Consistent padding (1.5rem)
- **Radius:** 20px border radius

#### Forms
- **Inputs:** White background with light gray border, dark text
- **Focus:** Deep red border with gold shadow
- **Error:** Deep red border and error text
- **Success:** Green checkmark and confirmation text

#### Navigation
- **Logo:** Primary SVG logo, left-aligned
- **Items:** Montserrat 600 weight, dark text
- **Active state:** Deep red underline or background
- **Mobile:** Hamburger menu with logo visible

## 9. Dos and Don'ts Summary

### DO ✅
- Use the provided logo files (SVG format)
- Maintain consistent color usage across touchpoints
- Keep ample clear space around logos
- Use Montserrat for English, Noto Sans Devanagari for Nepali
- Apply brand colors intentionally and sparingly
- Include both English and Nepali text where appropriate
- Test all materials for accessibility (WCAG AAA)
- Use authentic, respectful imagery of Nepal/children

### DON'T ❌
- Recreate, redraw, or modify the logo
- Change brand colors without approval
- Use decorative fonts for body text
- Mix multiple accent colors in one section
- Use exploitative or stereotypical imagery
- Ignore cultural sensitivity when depicting Nepal
- Assume all text can be gold on white
- Create materials without considering accessibility
